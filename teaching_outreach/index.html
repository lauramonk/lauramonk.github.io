Teaching

Current teaching

I am currently teaching a TCC course entitled Hyperbolic surfaces, their length spectra, and connections to Markoff triples with Luke Jeffreys.
The plan of the course is as follows, with links to the notes added progressively:

  1. The hyperbolic plane, its isometries and geodesics
  2. Hyperbolic surfaces and their length spectra
  3. Pair of pants decompositions, Fenchel-Nielsen coordinates and rigidity of the marked length spectrum
  4. Isospectral hyperbolic surfaces
  5. Multiplicities in the length spectrum and trace equalities
  6. Markoff triples and geodesics in hyperbolic tori
  7. A proof of McShane's identity via Markoff triples
  8. Counting Markoff triples and counting simple closed geodesics on hyperbolic tori

Outreach

The "Rendez-vous des Jeunes Mathématiciennes" is an event promoting mathematics toward high-school girls, organized by Animath and Femmes et Mathématiques. During three days, a group of 20-25 girls:

  • work on a research problem, under the supervision of PhD students, and present their results at the end of the stay
  • meet individually with professional women in mathematics and science
  • attend a few lectures by female mathematicians
  • are educated about gender stereotypes in science and mathematics.

The idea is for them to learn about careers in mathematics and science, meet some possible role models as well as other girls who love mathematics, and have fun doing maths!

I first discovered this event in Paris, and then organized it in the University of Strasbourg in 2018 and 2019, with Marie Chion.
